ART INSTITUTE OF
CHICAGO

The Art Institute of Chicago is a world-renowned museum and art school located in the heart of downtown Chicago, Illinois. Founded in 1879, it is one of the oldest and largest art museums in the United States, with a collection of over 300,000 works of art spanning over 5,000 years of human history. The museum is particularly well-known for its extensive holdings of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist paintings, including works by Monet, Renoir, and Van Gogh, as well as its comprehensive collections of American art, ancient art, and contemporary art. In addition to its collections, the Art Institute is also home to a thriving research library, conservation department, and numerous educational programs, making it a hub for scholarship and artistic practice in the Midwest and beyond.
